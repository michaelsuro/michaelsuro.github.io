About

I am a full stack iOS developer and mobile designer that has independently delivered four different applications to the App Store. Prior to building mobile apps I was the Director of Operations at Venga (acquired by Booking Holdings Inc.). At Venga I managed a team of operations specialists and worked shoulder to shoulder with developers in a product manager capacity. I began to teach myself how to code so I could become a more effective leader, and on the way bought a $9.99 video course on iOS development that changed everything. On a solo trip to India in 2015 I decided to quit my job and pursue iOS development full time. After nearly a year of teaching myself day and night I released Postable. The following month I was connected to an influencer in the fashion and events space in New York City who had a vision for a networking app, less than a year later we had raised a $200k round of seed financing and released Cinq. As Cinq started to wind down in the fall of 2019 I recovered by working on a passion project purely for fun called The Cities. Nowadays I’m developing apps as a contractor, supporting my latest project Flip flashcard maker, and immersing myself in the world of SwiftUI.

Work

Flip - Flashcard Maker & Study Tool

Flip allows you to create and organize engaging, beautiful flashcards that are synced across all of your iOS devices. Study by vertically scrolling your cards like a social media feed, or by swiping cards left and right like a dating app (right when learned, left to study again). Share your your cards with classmates or friends over email, messages, or AirDrop.

flipcardsapp.com

BirdsEye Bird Finding Guide

BirdsEyeBirding is one of the leading bird watching apps. When I took on the project the UI had not been updated since before AutoLayout was introduced, I performed one major overhaul and now I continue to make regular fixes and updates on the product as a contractor.

birdseyebirding.com

The Cities

I built The Cities in between larger projects as an excuse to pick up a few new skills (MVVM-C, SailsMVC backend), and work on an idea I couldn’t let go of. If you are located in NYC or LA with your phone, you can post a body of text to the city-wide feed on the app and it will be tagged with your neighborhood. You can segment posts by city and by neighborhood, and reply to posts with your neighborhood tagged. It’s completely anonymous.

The Cities on the App Store

Cinq

Cinq began as contact sharing and networking platform, and evolved into a group management and group messaging platform. Cinq provided a suite of features to ease the friction of networking such as introductions, overlapping travel dates, geo-fenced groups, admin managed group messaging channels, and connecting with others nearby over bluetooth. (No longer supported)

Postable

Postable is the best way to find out which photo you should post next. Get real time polling on which 1 of 2 photos to post, but in order to rack up votes, you yourself have to contribute to the voting of other people’s photos. Unfortunately Postable is no longer supported due to violating App Store guidelines 1.2.
